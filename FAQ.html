Frequently Asked Questions

What is online banking?

Online banking is a safe and secure service offered by our bank that allows customers to conduct various banking transactions over the internet, such as checking account balances, transferring funds, paying bills, and applying for loans.

What are the requirements for opening a new account?

To open a new account with Regal Bank, you will need to provide valid government-issued photo identification (such as a driver's license or passport) and proof of address (such as a recent utility bill or bank statement).

How do I transfer funds between accounts?

To transfer funds between your Regal Bank accounts, simply log in to your online banking account and select the "Transfer Funds" option. From there, you can choose the accounts you wish to transfer funds between and specify the amount.

What should I do if I suspect fraud on my account?

If you suspect fraudulent activity on your Regal Bank account, please contact us immediately by phone at 1-800-123-4567 or by visiting one of our branches. We will work with you to investigate the issue and take any necessary steps to protect your account. Here are some measures to take to protect your financial information: Don’t use public Wi-Fi or public computers for online banking. Use a strong password that can’t be easily guessed for your online banking account. Don’t share your user IDs, passwords and PINs. Don’t give your personal information to your financial institution by email. Don’t respond to suspicious messages or click on links within them.

What is the CDIC?

The CDIC stands for the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation. It is a federal Crown corporation in Canada that was established in 1967. CDIC provides deposit insurance to protect depositors in case their bank, credit union or other financial institution fails. If the Regal Bank institution fails, the CDIC will reimburse depositors for their insured deposits, up to a maximum of $100,000 per insured category (such as savings accounts, chequing account).
